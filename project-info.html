Redesign Mis Olas (My Waves) App

UI/UX Case Study

Disclaimer: This case study was done to enhance my learning experience and challenge me to redesign it. I was not paid by Mis Olas to redesign their app.

Introduction

I started surfing around 7 years ago and since that first moment when I stepped for the first time on my surfboard, I fell in love with this sport. That’s why for my first UI/UX case study I wanted to redesign this App because I feel it is a concept that I know about and I think I have some ideas to implement, including changing the design, which would help it grow even more.

About 6 months ago, my boyfriend found the application Mis Olas (My waves) and it changed the way we normally search for waves to surf. Before finding this app, we used to look at the forecast in other apps and trust them. Sometimes, the surf report wasn’t very precise, so we would drive for like an hour to a beach, only to find that the waves were too small or that the conditions were bad. Instead, we would have to drive back to our home or drive to nearby beaches to find a wave to surf.

Now, what we do firstly, is to check the cameras and then decide which beach we want to surf It saves us a lot of time, we no longer have to go all the way down to a beach and surprise ourselves with bad surfing conditions. Also, we can check how many people are surfing and decide if it is worth it to go at that moment.

What is Mis Olas (My waves)?

Mis Olas (My waves) is an App that shows, using real-time cameras, how the waves are in that exact moment. It was launched in 2021 for Peruvian surfers so they could find the best wave to surf that same day. Also, to save time in looking for a beach where the wave is in good condition. Right now, the app has 20 real-time cameras in 20 different waves found in the Peruvian coastal area. You can also check the forecast 10 days ahead, look at the events in 20 different places, use Multicam to check two spots at the same time, use a fast review to check the latest 10 minutes of the wave, and a reporting bot that is going to tell you how the waves are in real-time.
