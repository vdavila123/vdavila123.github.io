Redesign Mis Olas (My Waves) App

UI/UX Case Study

Disclaimer: This case study was done to enhance my learning experience and challenge me to redesign it. I was not paid by Mis Olas to redesign their app.

Introduction

I started surfing around 7 years ago and since that first moment when I stepped for the first time on my surfboard, I fell in love with this sport. That’s why for my first UI/UX case study I wanted to redesign this App because I feel it is a concept that I know about and I think I have some ideas to implement, including changing the design, which would help it grow even more.

About 6 months ago, my boyfriend found the application Mis Olas (My waves) and it changed the way we normally search for waves to surf. Before finding this app, we used to look at the forecast in other apps and trust them. Sometimes, the surf report wasn’t very precise, so we would drive for like an hour to a beach, only to find that the waves were too small or that the conditions were bad. Instead, we would have to drive back to our home or drive to nearby beaches to find a wave to surf.

Now, what we do firstly, is to check the cameras and then decide which beach we want to surf It saves us a lot of time, we no longer have to go all the way down to a beach and surprise ourselves with bad surfing conditions. Also, we can check how many people are surfing and decide if it is worth it to go at that moment.

What is Mis Olas (My waves)?

Mis Olas (My waves) is an App that shows, using real-time cameras, how the waves are in that exact moment. It was launched in 2021 for Peruvian surfers so they could find the best wave to surf that same day. Also, to save time in looking for a beach where the wave is in good condition. Right now, the app has 20 real-time cameras in 20 different waves found in the Peruvian coastal area. You can also check the forecast 10 days ahead, look at the events in 20 different places, use Multicam to check two spots at the same time, use a fast review to check the latest 10 minutes of the wave, and a reporting bot that is going to tell you how the waves are in real-time.

Why redesign?

I love the concept of Mis Olas App, but I found it was lacking in the UI and UX behind it. I think the visual perspective could be improved a lot because a part from resolving a problem for surfers, the way it looks can also provide surfers with the confidence they need to buy the App. Also there are animations that confuse me, more than help and guide me, on where to go next. I also think there could be more options to implement, and some that should be removed, since they aren’t so necessary. Now, I might not know the exact stats about how well the app is doing or how many users are using it, but I am sure that a redesign in which what I just mentioned could be implemented would help grow much bigger than what it is now. Hope you like it!

Goals

1. Improve customer experience on the app by improving the UI, UX and layout. That way the user will be able to understand perfectly how it works and will not get confused about using it also, make the customer want to buy the App.

2. Increase the information shown about the waves so, that every surfer has all the information they need, to know that they are going to catch the best available wave.

Hypothesis and Problem statement

Potential surfers want…

  • To check the waves forecast. (Mis Olas has this)
  • To check the weather forecast. (Mis Olas has this)
  • To see how good a wave is at that exact moment. (Mis Olas has this)
  • A way to communicate in real-time with other surfers to see their reviews of a wave. (Mis Olas doesn’t have this)
  • A way to know how to get to a beach (Mis Olas doesn’t have this)
  • To find different restaurants or hotels near the beach to eat or spend the night (Mis Olas doesn’t have this)
  • To be able to know which waves are in perfect conditions to surf (Mis Olas has this)
  • To get information about the wave (is it for beginners/ intermediate/ advanced surfers? Is it a sand break or a rocky break? In which conditions do they break perfectly? etc) (Mis Olas doesn’t have this)
  • To save their favorite waves (Mis Olas doesn’t have this)
  • A type of alert that tells them that the wave they love has perfect conditions (Mis Olas doesn’t have this)
  • A platform that is easy to use (Mis Olas has this)

    Research Methods
    The Survey

    To start my research I surveyed 39 Peruvian surfers. I asked them 4 questions to find out:

    1. Which apps or websites do you use the most to check the surf report?

    2. Do you know any local (Peruvian) app to check the surf report?

    3. Do you know about the Mis Olas (My waves) app?

    4. What do you expect to find in a surf app?

    What I found out:

    1. The platforms that are more used to check the surf report are Magicseaweed, Surfline, and Windguru.

    2. 51,3% didn’t know Mis Olas (My waves) App.

    3. There is another local platform to check the surf report that is called SurfPlace Peru.

    4. In the last question surfers would also like:

  • Comments in real-time
  • A map to know how to get to the beach
  • Information about the wave and the beach
  • A way to check nearby hotels or restaurants
  • A way to get notified or alerted if their favorite waves are in perfect conditions to surf
  • Let you safe your favorite waves
  • A way to contact local photographers and surf teachers
  • A way to know how many people are at the beach surfing in real-time.
  • A way to look at the forecast, as an alert, every morning.
